**Problem:** Determine the worst-case minimal distortion required to embed an n-point diversity into l1 (the diversity analogue of the classical metric embedding into l1). **Win condition:** Produce one or more explicit, machine-checkable certificates for small n (e.g. n ≤ 8–12): either (A) a diversity instance together with a proof/certificate (embedding map into l1 and computed distortion) showing a new higher lower bound on worst-case distortion for that n, or (B) an explicit embedding algorithm and concrete embeddings achieving a strictly better upper bound for some n. **Still open / closest prior:** open=True — Closest prior is Bryant–Tupper’s finite characterization: finite L1-embeddable diversities are exactly nonnegative combinations of cut/split diversities, and their hypergraph-flow section defines k1(δ), the minimal distortion to an L1-embeddable diversity, with k1(H)=γ(H). That gives the right LP/dual language but not small-n extremal values or certificate tables. Their open-problem note still gives only O(n) general upper and Ω(log n) inherited metric lower bounds, with special classes such as Steiner, diameter, partition/symmetric diversities treated separately. Jozefiak–Shepherd 2023 is the closest post-2017 update: it improves diameter diversities to O(log n), keeps arbitrary diversities at O(n), and proves an algorithmic/query lower barrier rather than determining f(n). ([dmtcs.episciences.org](https://dmtcs.episciences.org/2080/pdf)) ([dmtcs.episciences.org](https://dmtcs.episciences.org/2080/pdf)) ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1712.01960)) ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2303.04199))

**Why not already done:** It survived because the asymptotic problem is genuinely hard, but the small-n certificate problem falls between communities: metric-embedding people want O(log n) vs O(n), hypergraph-flow people use the LP gap abstractly, and polyhedral-computation people note the cut-diversity cone without optimizing distortion. The LP for a fixed diversity is straightforward; the hard part is finding a non-obvious diversity and producing rational primal/dual certificates. This is nontrivial but not hopeless because for fixed n everything is finite-dimensional: variables are subset-values δ(A), L1 candidates are cut weights w_U, and optimality is a standard rational LP dual/Farkas certificate.

Sanity-check by deriving f(3)=3/2, then enumerate or sample extreme rays of the 4- and 5-point diversity cones; after that run OpenEvolve/random LP-objective search for n=6–10 lower-bound instances and freeze the best rational certificate.

**Key risk:** The most likely sink is not verification but publishability: a certificate for n=3/4 only, or an example from a known symmetric/diameter/Steiner class, will be dismissed as toy/covered. ## [AMBER · MAYBE] arxiv-openproblem:2110.09638v3#2 (composite 4.5, openness conf medium)
**Problem:** For a multiple-access channel with n symmetric transmitters who only receive limited feedback each slot (e.g. ternary: idle/success/collision, or noisy/coarser versions of the full count F[t]), find symmetric decentralized transmission strategies that minimize the expected time until the first success (channel capture). Characterize the achievable expected capture time as a function of n and the feedback model, and quantify how performance degrades as feedback is reduced.

**Win condition:** Produce an explicit symmetric decentralized protocol (possibly randomized with bounded memory or simple adaptive rule) together with a rigorous, checkable certificate for its expected time-to-first-success as a function of n and the feedback model, and either (a) prove a matching lower bound showing optimality within a stated policy class (e.g. memory-k, or symmetric randomized stationary policies), or (b) provide a computer-assisted certificate (exhaustive/branch-and-bound search over the policy class plus computed expected times) that the protocol is optimal for all n up to some moderate N and asymptotically optimal (with explicit constants) for large n. For ternary feedback a successful artifact could be a closed-form optimal policy in the memoryless or 1-step memory class with provable expected capture-time bounds and a short proof or verified computation.

**Still open / closest prior:** open=True — Closest is Neely’s full-count-feedback channel-capture problem: exact/optimal for n∈{1,2,3,4,6}, conjectured algorithm for all n, and explicit open question asking what is optimal with more limited feedback; the paper stresses that full F[t] is more detailed than idle/success/collision for n>2. ([allerton.csl.illinois.edu](https://allerton.csl.illinois.edu/files/2022/12/2022-115-paper_4748.pdf)) Classical ternary-feedback work is mainly asymptotic selection/contention: Willard/QSEB gives log log n+o(log log n) expected selection with collision detection when n is unknown/unbounded, not finite-n exact optimal expected capture for known anonymous symmetric users. ([link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00446-016-0265-z)) The n=2 exact front is largely closed in an even weaker acknowledgement-only model, but that does not resolve n>2 ternary because private post-collision types appear. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.08845))

**Why not already done:** It survived because the natural exact problem is a decentralized partially observed team problem, not an ordinary MDP: after a ternary collision, transmitters and non-transmitters have different private information, while the public signal does not reveal the collider count. Neely explicitly notes an exponential/‘worse’ complexity explosion and no known polynomial-time general solution even in the more informative full-count setting; the classical limited-feedback literature optimizes asymptotic selection/wakeup/throughput rather than exact finite-n expectation. ([allerton.csl.illinois.edu](https://allerton.csl.illinois.edu/files/2022/12/2022-115-paper_4748.pdf)) This is not derivable-on-sight from slotted ALOHA: the memoryless optimum p=1/n and value (1-1/n)^{-(n-1)} is immediate, but any nontrivial adaptive theorem must certify optimality over hidden-type histories or over a sharply defined restricted policy class.

**1-week recommendation:** MAYBE (engine both)

**First concrete step:** Day 1: narrow brutally to “known n, ternary feedback, stationary one-step private-memory policies.” Encode the policy by p for the homogeneous state and x,y after a collision for users whose previous action was transmit/non-transmit; build the exact finite Markov chain on states H and collision-count states j=2,…,n, with transition probabilities from Bin(j,x)+Bin(n-j,y), and compute E_n(p,x,y) as a rational function by solving (I-Q)v=1. Then run interval branch-and-bound over [0,1]^3 for n=3,…,N, producing rational interval certificates and candidate closed forms/asymptotics. If a clean n=3 theorem emerges, try an all-policy lower bound there; otherwise publish only the certified restricted-class result.

**Key risk:** The likely sink is not finding a protocol but certifying a lower bound strong enough to be worth publishing: global optimization over the continuous policy parameters is manageable, but optimality outside a very narrow memory-1 stationary class will probably require new decentralized-control inequalities, and the narrow-class result may be judged too artificial unless it yields a clean formula or a substantial improvement over memoryless.
